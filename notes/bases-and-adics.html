Bases & Adics

Last updated: Sep 11, 2025draft
A friendly walk from ordinary number bases to the p-adic world.

1. What changes when a base changes?

Writing 349 in base-10 is secretly 3·10² + 4·10 + 9. Swap 10 for b and you’re in base-b.

  • Digits. 0…b−1
  • Place values. … , b³, b², b, 1, b⁻¹, …

2. Flip the telescope: the p-adic idea

Fix prime p. Measure size by divisibility: more factors of p ⇒ smaller. Define v_p(n) and |n|_p = p^{-v_p(n)}.

Strong triangle inequality: |x+y|_p ≤ max(|x|_p,|y|_p).

3. p-adic expansions look backwards

Reals converge when right tails stabilize. p-adics converge when left digits in base p stabilize. Every p-adic integer has a unique expansion a₀ + a₁p + a₂p² + ….

4. Constructing ℚp

Complete ℚ under d_p(x,y)=|x−y|_p to get ℚp. The closed unit ball is ℤp.

4.1 Units and Hensel

In ℤp, anything not divisible by p is a unit. Simple roots mod p lift to ℤp (Hensel).

5. Tiny examples

5.1 Geometric series “the wrong way”

In 5-adics, 1+5+5²+…=1/(1-5)=-1/4 since |5|_5=1/5<1.

5.2 The “…222” trick

In 3-adics, …222 = -1 because 2/(1-3)=-1.

6. Reals vs. p-adics

  • Size: magnitude vs. divisibility
  • Convergence: right vs. left stabilization
  • Inequality: usual vs. ultrametric

7. Where this goes

Hensel lifting, LTE-style arguments, and local–global ideas. p-adics are base-thinking with a new notion of “closeness”.
